Lipid-protein interactions
Lipid-protein interactions play pivotal roles in biological membranes. We used MD simulations and crystallographic refinement to study the localization of DMPC lipids (blue) around AQP0 (white). We found that the positions of the constrained lipids in the 2D crystals determined by electron crystallography together with their B-factors are representative of the behavior of unconstrained lipids surrounding individual AQP0 tetramers. We conclude that positions of DMPC lipids around AQP0 are defined by the acyl chains rather than the head-groups. Furthermore, we observed that the positions of the lipids are largely influenced by the local mobility of the protein, but interestingly not vice versa. Specific hydrogen bonds appear on the contrary to play a secondary role. Moreover, our simulations support a general mechanism in which membrane proteins laterally diffuse accompanied by several layers of localized lipids (blue and green), with the positions of the annular lipids being influenced the most by the protein surface.
